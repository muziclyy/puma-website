Indian Defence System

Led by Palak under Government

The Indian Defence System is undergoing a historic transformation in 2025, marked as the ‘Year of Reforms’ by the Ministry of Defence. India is focusing on modernizing its armed forces, integrating new technologies, and strengthening indigenous manufacturing to ensure national security and global competitiveness.

  • Rollout of the Tejas MkII advanced fighter jet prototype.
  • Strengthening indigenous drone and UAV programs for surveillance and combat.
  • Implementation of Integrated Theatre Commands to unify Army, Navy, and Air Force operations.
  • Acquisition of advanced weapons like BrahMos missiles, Pinaka rocket launchers, and Prachand helicopters.
  • Focus on cyber and space warfare, AI, and robotics for future-readiness.
  • Public-private partnerships and support for defence startups under the Atmanirbhar Bharat initiative.

These reforms and innovations ensure that India remains secure, self-reliant, and ready to face modern challenges.
